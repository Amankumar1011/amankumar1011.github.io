Centre for Science and Environment

The Centre for Science and Environment (CSE) is a non-profit research and advocacy organization based in New Delhi, India. It was established in 1980 by the environmentalist Anil Agarwal and is one of the leading environmental research and advocacy organizations in India. CSE works on a range of environmental issues, including air pollution, water pollution, climate change, and sustainable development. They conduct research, provide technical assistance, and advocate for policies that promote sustainable development and reduce environmental impacts. In the context of air pollution, CSE has been actively working towards finding solutions to reduce air pollution levels in India, with a particular focus on Delhi, which is one of the most polluted cities in the world. They have conducted extensive research on the sources and impacts of air pollution in Delhi and have proposed a range of policy solutions to address the issue. CSE has also been involved in capacity-building and awareness-raising initiatives aimed at educating policymakers, the media, and the public about the health impacts of air pollution and the need for urgent action to address this issue. They have also worked on promoting sustainable transportation options, such as cycling and walking, and have advocated for policies that promote clean energy and reduce emissions from vehicles and industries.
